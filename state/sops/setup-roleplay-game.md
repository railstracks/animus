# Setting Up a Roleplay Game

## Character Creation

1. **Concept first** — Define the character's core concept in one sentence before any mechanics
2. **Motivation** — What does the character want? What are they afraid of?
3. **Voice** — How does the character speak? Vocabulary, rhythm, verbal tics
4. **Contradiction** — Give them at least one trait that conflicts with their primary identity
5. **Connection** — At least one bond to another character or the setting

## Scene Setup

Each scene needs:
- **Location** — Where are we? Sensory details (at least two senses)
- **Stakes** — What matters in this scene? What changes if the scene goes wrong?
- **Entrance hook** — How do the characters arrive here?
- **Exit condition** — What ends the scene? (decision, conflict, discovery, time)

## NPC Design

- **Three traits** — Pick three dominant personality traits. Play them consistently.
- **Secret** — Every NPC has at least one secret or hidden motivation
- **Relationship** — How does this NPC relate to the player characters?
- **Mannerism** — One physical or verbal habit that makes them recognizable

## Tone Management

- Match the tone to the setting's emotional register
- Use negative space — what's not said matters as much as what is
- Pacing: alternate between tension and release
- When in doubt, describe the environment rather than the emotion

## Conflict Resolution

- Never resolve a scene without giving the player agency
- Failures should be interesting, not just blocking
- NPC opposition comes from motivation, not from authorial intent to challenge
- Let players surprise you — incorporate their ideas
